Origins

Buddhism was founded over 2,500 years ago in ancient India by Siddhartha Gautama, who became known as the Buddha, meaning "the awakened one." Born a prince, he renounced his luxurious life to seek the truth about suffering and the path to liberation.

üåø

Philosophy

Buddhism is both a religion and a philosophy that offers a practical path to understanding the nature of reality, reducing suffering, and achieving inner peace. It emphasizes personal spiritual development and the attainment of deep insight into the true nature of life.

üåç

Global Reach

Today, Buddhism is practiced by over 500 million people worldwide. It has spread across Asia and beyond, adapting to different cultures while maintaining its core teachings of wisdom, compassion, and mindfulness.

Core Teachings

The Four Noble Truths

  • Dukkha: Life involves suffering
  • Samudaya: Suffering has causes
  • Nirodha: Suffering can end
  • Magga: There is a path to end suffering

The Noble Eightfold Path

  • Right Understanding
  • Right Thought
  • Right Speech
  • Right Action
  • Right Livelihood
  • Right Effort
  • Right Mindfulness
  • Right Concentration

Key Principles

  • Karma: Actions have consequences
  • Rebirth: Cycle of existence
  • Impermanence: All things change
  • Non-self: No permanent self
  • Compassion: Loving-kindness for all

Buddhist Practices

Meditation

Meditation is central to Buddhist practice. It helps develop mindfulness, concentration, and insight. Common forms include mindfulness meditation (Vipassana) and concentration meditation (Samatha), which calm the mind and develop awareness.

üôè

Mindfulness

Mindfulness involves paying attention to the present moment with awareness and acceptance. It's practiced throughout daily activities, helping practitioners develop greater awareness of their thoughts, feelings, and actions.

üìñ

Study of Dharma

Studying Buddhist teachings (Dharma) helps deepen understanding of the path. This includes reading sutras, listening to teachings, and reflecting on the wisdom of the Buddha and other enlightened masters.

üíù

Compassionate Action

Buddhism emphasizes putting wisdom into action through compassionate deeds. This includes helping others, practicing generosity, and living ethically according to the precepts.
